Connecting Silverstone


We're not happy about Silverstone's connection to BT Openreach's fibre network. Most homes can't get connected to fibre broadband, even if they are close to the cabinet.
So we want to set up our own village network for the modern internet.

Why it matters



Media
To stream high quality video, you need at least 5Mbps download speed, and that's something standard copper broadband simply can't deliver.

Upload
Standard copper broadband only offers about half a megabit of upload speed. This was fine a few years ago, but we're uploading more and more every day. For example, when syncing files to Dropbox, uploading photos to Facebook, putting videos on YouTube, or having a Skype video call, your upload speed matters.

Reliability
Standard broadband relies on copper cables all the way from the BT exchange to homes and business premises. The signal degrades over time, which means that it reduces speeds and makes the broadband signal susceptible to interference.
